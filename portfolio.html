How did I create this website?

I created this website from scratch using HTML and CSS to style the pages.

I followed this YouTube tutorial, from which I tweaked and added my personal touch.

Features that I added include:

    - the accordions used on this Portfolio page, - images in the first row under Artwork with hover effects that pop out and display a caption when you click on them - the quiz I created for an assignment in my Intro to Computing course in the "For Fun" section

The site is being hosted on Github.

The first time I dabbled with Adobe Illustrator was as a graphic design team member at Watonomous - an autonomous vehicle student design team!

I also used it while redesigning a logo for eMentorship, where I was head of Marketing. eMentorship was a subdivision of the Internal Services (IS) Committee I joined in first year as a Project Manager and Executive Coordinator. IS is one of the committees in the Accounting and Financial Management Student Association.

I used Illustrator to design the graphics on this site, including the bao home logo in the navigation pane and the cartoon icon of myself on the homepage.

I wanted this website to be clean, and minimalistic but still have some personality. Initially, I thought that the typewriter style font would be fitting of the minimalist theme however I did debate whether a sleek, modern font would fit the look I am trying to achieve better. The font I used in "Hello" is Gluten, and the main font used here is Alata from the Google Fonts direcctory.

Why did I create this website?

I was inspired by the countless beautiful personal/CV websites I came across from friends in coding-related programs at the University of Waterloo. I wanted to learn how to design my own and play around with the fonts, placements, and features that are possible while also showing off a tangible product from my efforts.

Since I enjoyed creating art and taking photos outside of class, I also thought it could be a cool idea to have a central site to link everything together.

There's one final project that I'd like to work on before I graduate which will be coming soon...
